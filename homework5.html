Illustrated Environment Project

Original Image

Illustrated Version

Paragraph

I chose this environment because I was drawn to how peaceful and calm it felt in its original form. The scene has a quiet, almost still quality that makes it visually relaxing, which is what initially caught my attention. When I transformed it into an illustrated version, it was interesting to see how that same setting changed in mood. By simplifying shapes, exaggerating certain features, the image became less serene and more playful and stylized. The process of creating the illustration was time-consuming and detail-oriented. I relied heavily on the Pen Tool to trace and reconstruct most of the forms, which required a lot of focus and precision while working closely on the screen. I also organized the composition using separate layers for the foreground, middle ground, and background, which helped create depth and made the image easier to manage as I built it up. Choosing and applying a limited set of colors pushed me to think carefully about contrast and visual clarity, while still maintaining the overall composition. Throughout the process, I added small details and refined shapes to enhance the final result, turning the original photograph into a more stylized and expressive interpretation.
