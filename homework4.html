Homework 4: two-paragraph explanation of the concepts I explored and what techniques I employed

My photomontage explores the idea of enjoying the present moment in a culture that often prioritizes productivity and constant consumption. The composition features an airplane with the phrase “Enjoy Life” written behind it, inspired by the advertising planes that fly banners across beaches. I originally considered creating a more detailed banner being pulled behind the plane, but decided to keep the message directly on the airplane so that the concept remained clear and simple. I placed the scene over a beach background because the beach represents relaxation and happiness to me, and it symbolizes a space where people are able to slow down and enjoy life. In contrast, I placed an image of a man working at his desk partially submerged in the water to represent how many people become so focused on work and productivity that they lose sight of what is around them. This juxtaposition highlights the tension between work culture and the importance of appreciating the present moment. To create this photomontage, I used several Photoshop techniques including selections, layer masks, and blending modes. I imported multiple images into a single document using Place Embedded so the files remained Smart Objects and the editing process stayed non-destructive. I used the Object Selection Tool and Select Subject to isolate the airplane and the figure working at the desk, and then applied layer masks to remove the backgrounds and combine the images together. I refined the masks with a soft brush to clean up edges and control what parts of each image were visible. To integrate the elements more naturally into the beach scene, I experimented with blending modes and opacity adjustments so that the layers interacted with the background in a more cohesive way. I also used adjustment layers to help balance the lighting and colors across the composition. The images used in this project were sourced from online images and combined to create a collage-style composition that communicates the overall concept while still maintaining a non-destructive editing workflow.
